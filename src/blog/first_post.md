title: "Writing"
date: 2026-06-17
---

So I finally started a blog.

I am quite suprised it took this long, it's nice to have everything set up and ready to go,
now the only bottleneck is writing.

There is something a bit scary about putting yourself out there, for all the world to see.
Maybe 'all the world' is an exaggeration, but tell that to the primal desire to be safe and accepted.
It's very easy to stay comfortable all your life, you don't have to share, or commit, or create, or do anything that risks you getting hurt. 
That's something lots of people do, to an extent.
Pulling away from the world and deeper connection to be creatively a stranger, alone.
Nonetheless I think there is something valueable not only in writing, as both a tool and a form of expression, but in sharing your work as well.


Knowing that the words I am writing may be read by others creates a very different process compared to writing in a journal no one will read.
I am trying to formulate my ideas, keep pacing in mind, and share something I think is worth sharing.
The act of sharing creates a different journey which makes the output of my work more considered.
Part of me is also excited to be a part of the community which expresses themselves through ones and zeroes on the internet.


Writing is very useful to me as a tool.
As a tool it serves me by organising my thoughts and externalising my ideas.
In this form writing is like programming to me, the act of which allows me to see the errors in my mental model which supports some opinion. 
The programming analogy falls a bit short for the organisational mode of writing, where I write to escape thought loops and map out where I have been and where I need to go.


I am much less experienced at writing as a form of expression.
I haven't written essays, reviews, or anything of that nature since high school, and attempting again now has revealed to me just how much I have atrophied.
Writing doesn't just exist as a form of logic and plans, it can also be used as a way to amplify everything that makes you, you.
Having opinions on things, choosing the right word from a vocabulary, and deciding to write about some obscure game console is what makes a person and individual.
I think we live in a time where people would really benefit from expressing themselves more, me included.


Even if I never write another post I will be content in knowing I put a part of myself out there for all to see. 
I hope if you are reading this you can find a way to express yourself to the humans around you.
