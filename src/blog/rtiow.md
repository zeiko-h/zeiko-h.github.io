I saw the [Ray Tracing in One Weekend] (https://raytracing.github.io/books/RayTracingInOneWeekend.html) pop up somewhere and thought it would be an interesting project to follow along with.
It's been a very long time since I have done any form of linear algebra, and while this isn't a hyper complex linear algebra project, I felt a large amount of brain fog in doing this project.

This article is written for someone like me, who is interested in raytracing (enough to read a post about it at least) and who is a beginner at zig. The article does a much better job than I could ever do at explaining what is going on, so I will try explain any zig-ism I run into along the way, as well as maybe summarise the gist of whats going on.
